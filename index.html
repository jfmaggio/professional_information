Welcome to My Professional Profile

I'm a molecular biologist applying computational biology tools to the study of transcriptomics in celiac disease. My current work involves utilizing various bioinformatics techniques to analyze genetic data and gain insights into this condition.

Current Projects

  • Analyzing transcriptomic data related to celiac disease to identify key genetic variants and biomarkers.
  • Developing pipelines for data processing and visualization to enhance understanding of gene and isoform expression patterns.

Aspirations

At this moment, I am working as a doctoral fellow at the GIBAIB UNLu, focusing on the transcriptomics of celiac disease. In 2024, I began my Specialization in Bioinformatics at UNLu. My aspiration is to become a bioinformatician, where I can further expand my skills in data analysis and computational biology. My goal is to bridge the gap between biology and technology to contribute to advancements in health and disease research.

Tools and Technologies

  • R, Python, and Bash scripting.
  • SRAToolkit for downloading sequences.
  • Trim Galore, FastQC, and NGSQCToolkit for quality control of sequences.
  • Trinity, NEWBLER, and SOAPdenovo-Trans for de novo transcriptome assembly and StringTie for reference-based assembly and GTF merging.
  • BUSCO, rnaQUAST, and DETONATE for post-assembly quality control.
  • STAR for sequence mapping.
  • BCFtools for manipulating VCF files.
  • GATK for searching for short genetic variants in RNA-Seq.
  • SnpEff for variant annotation.
  • Ballgown for differential isoform expression analysis.
